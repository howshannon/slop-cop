# Research sources and scope

Use research as calibration, not as a universal authorship detector. Linguistic
patterns vary by model, prompt, language, genre, and editing process. Prefer
observable quality findings over claims about provenance.

## Prose

- Survey of linguistic characteristics of AI-generated text:
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.11903
- Large-scale analysis of linguistic signals in human and AI text:
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.03283

## Code

- Code smells in LLM-generated code:
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.08269
- Security weaknesses and validation/error-handling concerns in generated code:
  https://owasp.org/www-project-top-10-for-large-language-model-applications/

## Design

- Hallmark’s catalog of generated-UI patterns:
  https://github.com/Nutlope/hallmark
- Research on dark patterns in AI-generated interfaces:
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.11248

## Rule discipline

Do not include model-version claims, fixed sentence-length thresholds,
“strongest tell” claims, or numeric behavior ratios unless the exact source,
population, and limitations are linked. Rules should survive model updates.
